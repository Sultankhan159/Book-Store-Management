Popular Books

"To Kill a Mockingbird" by Harper Lee

Set in the racially charged American South during the 1930s, this novel follows young Scout Finch as she navigates her childhood and learns about morality, justice, and empathy through her father's defense of a black man falsely accused of raping a white woman.

"1984" by George Orwell

A dystopian novel that explores the dangers of totalitarianism, surveillance, and censorship. It follows Winston Smith, a man living under an oppressive regime that manipulates truth and controls every aspect of life, as he seeks to rebel against the Party and find individuality.

"The Great Gatsby" by F. Scott Fitzgerald

Set in the Roaring Twenties, this novel tells the story of Jay Gatsby, a wealthy and mysterious man who throws extravagant parties in hopes of rekindling a past romance with Daisy Buchanan. It explores themes of love, wealth, and the American Dream, ultimately revealing the emptiness behind the glittering facade of society.
